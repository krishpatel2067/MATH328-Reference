\section{Fourier Series}

\subsection{Introduction}

Given a $2L$-periodic function $f(x)$, its Fourier series is given by

\begin{equation*}
    f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^\infty \left[ a_n \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) + b_n \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \right]
\end{equation*}

where

\begin{align*}
    a_n & = \frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^L f(x)\cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)~dx         \\
    b_n & = \frac{1}{L}\int_{-L}^L f(x)\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)~dx\text{.}
\end{align*}

Note that the $a_n$ formula applies for $a_0$ too with $\cos(0) = 1$.

\vsp

The convergence of the Fourier series relies heavily on the coefficients $a_n$ and $b_n$ since the trigonometric functions simply oscillate between $-1$ and $1$.
